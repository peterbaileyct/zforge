# Z-World
A Z-Forge World specification is a Z-Bundle used for [RAG](RAG%20and%20GRAG%20Implementation.md). The type slug for it is "world". It contains the following elements:

## Raw Text
The original input text will be kept so that the other elements can be regenerated by later versions of the app as new features are added, e.g. new node types to look for and a more detailed ETL process.

**Storage:** Written to `{root}/source.txt` as UTF-8 plain text by `ZWorldManager.create()`.

## Key-Value
- Title (human-readable, full text, e.g. "Discworld")
- slug (dash-case, unique among Z-Worlds on the same device, e.g. "lord-of-the-rings")
- UUID
- Summary (1-3 paragraphs of plain text describing the world in diegetic terms, used to help unfamiliar users to pick a world for experience generation and to help kick-start experience generation, especially where no prompt is given)
- Setting era (brief label for when the world is nominally set, e.g. "pre-industrial fantasy", "far future", "alternate 1920s"; used as a quick LLM hint during experience generation)
- Source canon (list of source work titles — books, films, games — the world is drawn from; used for disambiguation between continuities and to guide the document parsing pipeline)
- Content advisories (list of thematic flags relevant to experience generation, e.g. "moderate violence", "political intrigue", "body horror"; informs tone guidance and content guardrails)

## Vector
Lists of:
- Characters
- Locations (at any granularity, as long as there is a significance to the location, e.g. "Sto Plains", "Lancre", and "Ankh-Morpork", but also "Ramkin House", "Dragon Pens", and "Chubby's Pen").
- Events (includes a description and a time)
- Factions / Organizations (guilds, governments, armies, cults, secret societies, and other named groups with collective agency, e.g. "the Thieves' Guild", "the Night Watch", "the Fellowship of the Ring")
- Artifacts / Items (significant named objects that exist as instances in the world, distinct from mechanics; e.g. "the One Ring", "Carrot's sword", "the Luggage")
- Eras / Time Periods (named historical ages or epochs used to anchor Events and world history, e.g. "the Third Age", "Before the Schism", "the Interregnum")
- Cultures (distinct cultural identities that do not map cleanly to a single species or location, e.g. "Dothraki", "Klatchian")
- Deities / Cosmic Entities (gods, demons, and primordial beings with special ontological status in the world; may overlap with Characters but their scope and nature typically differs)
- Prophecies / Texts (in-world documents, prophecies, scriptures, and significant writings that characters or events relate to)
- Concepts / Abstract Forces (named metaphysical things that exist in the world but are not rules; e.g. "the Dark", "the Balance", "Fate" — distinct from Mechanics, which describe *rules* of the world)
- Mechanics (e.g. "magic exists and its use is divided between academic but often silly wizards and rural, under-respected witches who function largely as doctors and midwives")
- Tropes (both for story elements and narrative style, e.g. "stories often feature found families" and "frequent, long footnotes detailing world history and languages")
- Species (if absent, assumed to map to Earth species)
- Occupations (may be real-world occupations often emphasized in the fictional world, or invented occupations specific to it)

## Property Graph
Describes relationships between the above elements in the vector store. Examples include, but are not limited to:

**Character — Character**
{character} friends_with {character}
{character} enemy_of {character}
{character} parent_of {character}
{character} mentor_of {character}

**Character — World**
{character} present_at {event}
{character} born_in {location}
{character} member_of {faction}
{character} leads {faction}
{character} is_a {species}
{character} is_a {occupation}
{character} owns {item}
{character} seeks {item}
{character} subject_to {prophecy}
{character} embodies {concept}

**Location — Location**
{location} west_of {location}
{location} inside_of {location}

**Location — World**
{faction} controls {location}
{species} native_to {location}
{item} located_at {location}
{event} occurred_at {location}

**Faction — Faction**
{faction} allied_with {faction}
{faction} at_war_with {faction}

**Event — Event**
{event} caused {event}

**Era — Era**
{era} preceded {era}
